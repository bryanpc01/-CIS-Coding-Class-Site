Chapter 1, Exercise 1.13

Three benefits of cloud computing:

Imagine being assigned a group research project. Cloud computing can make this project extremely manageable. For one, you can keep all documents in ONE place. This is nothing special until you factor in each member of the group being able to access elements of the document anywhere and anytime. No need to meet at the library; no need to wait for the next day of class; all you need is an internet connection and you are on your way. The cloud gives group members the opportunity to edit files simultaneously from different locations. And lastly, with cloud computing you do not have to worry about losing saved documents because your flash drive fell in a puddle.

As a freshman in high school I slipped and fell on a (invisible) puddle. My flash drive feel into a puddle and I lost all of my assignments.

Chapter 1, Exercise 1.14

If I were to merge Skype and YouTube:

My web services application would combine the powers of YouTube and Skype. I am very much into collaborative learning. I have learned people think and behave differently in different geological locations. My application would be one that turns living rooms all over the globe into one big classroom. Skype would be the service that connects the different students. Skype is basically the main component of the application. It will be the classroom. Discussions, chats, presentations will all be done with the powers of Skype. YouTube will be the lecture component of the classroom. All videos and assignments will be uploaded to YouTube. The great thing about YouTube is that anyone can access the information after the semester end.
